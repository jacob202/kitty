# Honda Ridgeline (2019-2022) — Known Issues

## 1. VTC Actuator Rattle on Cold Start

**Symptom:** Metallic rattle for 1–3 seconds immediately after cold start, especially below 5°C.
**Cause:** Variable Timing Control (VTC) actuator loses oil pressure overnight. Normal hydraulic fill delay.
**Verdict:** Normal on J35A9. Honda TSB exists. No repair needed unless rattle persists past 5 seconds or occurs when warm.
**Action:** None required. Use 0W-20 full synthetic. If rattle persists >5s, inspect VTC actuator solenoid screen for debris.

---

## 2. Bank 2 Exhaust Manifold Gasket Failure (Primary Known Issue)

**Symptom:** LTFT Bank 2 consistently elevated at idle (+3% to +10%). Stft B2 also elevated. Possible faint exhaust smell at idle. May hear faint tick near driver's side exhaust under hood.
**Cause:** Exhaust manifold gasket on Bank 2 (driver's side, front bank) fails due to thermal cycling. Small exhaust leak upstream of B2S1 O2 sensor. ECU sees false lean condition, adds fuel.
**Affected years:** Primarily 2019–2021. Some 2022 models also affected.
**DTC:** Usually none at first. May eventually set P0420 (cat efficiency B2) if left long enough.
**Diagnosis:** Listen for tick at idle near driver's side manifold. OBD confirmation: LTFT B2 >+5% at idle while B1 is normal (0–3%).
**Fix:** Replace exhaust manifold gasket (or full manifold if cracked). Some shops replace both sides while in there.
**Cost Regina SK:** $400–$800 CAD parts + labour. Independent shop preferred over dealer. Dealer may quote $1,000+.
**Parts:** Honda OEM manifold gasket set ~$60-120 CAD. Manifold itself ~$200-400 if needed.

## 3. Torque Converter Shudder (TC Shudder)

**Symptom:** Shudder or vibration felt through drivetrain/floor at light throttle, 60–90 km/h, when TC lock-up clutch engages.
**Cause:** Torque converter lock-up clutch friction material glazes over time. Worsened by old or degraded transmission fluid.
**First fix:** Drain and fill transmission fluid with Honda DW-1. This resolves ~70% of cases.
**Cost:** Fluid drain+fill ~$100-150 CAD at independent shop (do NOT use quick-lube).
**If fluid doesn't fix it:** TC replacement ~$1,500–$2,500 CAD. Avoid if possible — try fluid first twice.
**Note:** NEVER use non-Honda transmission fluid. Using the wrong fluid accelerates shudder.

---

## 4. TPMS Sensor Battery Failure

**Symptom:** TPMS warning light, sensor not reading (shows dashes or "---" for one wheel), possibly after 5+ years of ownership.
**Cause:** TPMS sensor batteries (CR2032 or similar internal) have ~5–7 year lifespan. Not replaceable — full sensor replacement required.
**Cost:** $50–$90 CAD per sensor, plus $20-30 programming fee per sensor. Often done when tires are swapped.
**Tip:** If you're already paying for tire mounting/balancing for winter/summer swap, bundle sensor replacement then.

---

## 5. i-VTM4 Rear Diff Actuator Noise

**Symptom:** Clunk or groan from rear on tight low-speed turns (parking lots, U-turns). Especially in cold weather.
**Cause:** Rear differential torque-vectoring clutches. Normal in cold conditions. May indicate dry/old rear diff fluid.
**Fix:** Rear differential fluid change (Honda DPS-F). Very cheap — usually included in any drivetrain service.

---

## 6. Cold Weather Notes (Regina-Specific)

- At -30°C to -40°C: expect rough idle for 2–5 minutes on cold start. Normal for VTEC engines.
- LTFT values unreliable until coolant reaches 80°C — don't interpret cold-start fuel trims.
- Battery load: stock battery is marginal at Regina winters. Consider AGM upgrade at 4–5 years.
- Block heater: use it. 2-hour pre-heat at -20°C or colder. Reduces cam chain wear dramatically.
- Road salt: undercarriage flush every spring is not optional in Regina. Ridgeline frames are vulnerable to salt packing in bed area.
